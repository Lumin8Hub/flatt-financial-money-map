What we need to confirm before the numbers are trustworthy

The bank feed shows cash out, never invoices. To turn the estimates above into real numbers, we need answers to these. Split into questions for Dorit (the arrangements) and for the bookkeeper (the ledger).

Ask Dorit — the contractor arrangements

Sydney Finn (Finn Digital). Is she a flat monthly fee, hourly, or a % of Yogen Fruz? What exact months has she been paid for, and how many months are currently un-invoiced? Confirm the ~$1,413/mo estimate and the total she's owed today.
Does Sydney do anything besides Yogen Fruz? Any other clients or the website work?
Ravil Muldagaliyev. What's his role and which client/work does he support? Is the ~$500/mo a retainer or per-task? Any unbilled balance?
Sohel Rana. Who is this and what do they do? (Small amounts via Wise suggest an overseas micro-contractor.) Ongoing? Expected monthly cost?
Any other contractors or vendors who invoice irregularly and may have unpaid balances we can't see in the bank feed?
Does Dorit invoice Lumin8 for her own time (e.g. the Acronym hours), or only take dividends? Is there a Dorit payable building up too?
Website line of business: are there per-project costs (designers, developers, stock assets) that should be netted against the ~$500 builds?

Ask the bookkeeper — the ledger

Total accounts payable today. What is the full balance of unpaid/unrecorded invoices as of now — the single most important number for trusting take-home.
Accounts receivable. Is any Acronym/Yogen Fruz/website revenue billed-but-unpaid? That would partly offset the payables.
HST. What's the current HST balance owing (collected minus input tax credits), and when is the next remittance due?
Corporate income tax. Are installments owing, and is there a reserve for tax on dividends drawn (none is withheld at source)?
Lumpy / annual costs not visible in the 3-month window — year-end accounting fees, annual software renewals, corporate filing, insurance.
Elaine's own arrangement. Flat monthly or hourly, and is she current?
Is Sydney Finn's cost accrued in the books as a payable, or only recorded when paid? (If the latter, every prior month's profit is overstated.)

Once these are answered we can lock the contractor run-rates, net the website revenue, and produce a take-home figure for Dorit/Lumin8 that's reliable rather than estimated — and decide a sustainable monthly draw that doesn't outrun what the business earns.
